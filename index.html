Getting Technical With Snowboards.

The first item on the list is snowboarding! I returned to snowboarding recently using a secondhand board that my father bought when he started snowboarding. Built sometime in the mid 90s. I could not find much detail on the board itself. However, with a length of 160cm, it was both too long and too stiff for the type of riding that I wanted to do. Towards the end of the 20/21 season. When end of season sales started. I picked up a new snowboard as well as bindings. Below is a table of the specs for the board for those more keen on those details.

Table 1: Snowboard Specifications
Brand Model Shape Profile Length (cm) Flex (/10) Effective Edge (mm) Waist (cm) Nose/Tail (cm) Sidecut (m) Max Stance (cm/in) Suggested Rider Weight (lbs/kg)
CAPiTA Outerspace Living 20/21 True Twin Resort V3 (Cam-Rock) 154 5 1170 24.8 29.1/29.1 7.90 63.5/25.0 120-180/54-81

Compared with my old board. The difference in length was definitely noticeable. The board retained less speed and I missed the stability of my previous board.
The bindings that I chose to pick up with the snowboard were the 20/21 Union Force Bindings. Combined, I really liked how locked in and reponsive the snowboard felt underfoot. However, with the stiffer bindings. I felt limited in my ability to press and "butter". As a work around, I have played around with not cranking my ankle straps all the way down to increase the amount of flex in the bindings. Allowing me to more easily project my weight out over my nose and tail. Now I do not necessarily recommend you do the same as I can't guarantee there being risks that I haven't realized. But it can be something you could look to consider trying if this is an issue you're running into.


The Snowboard

So what do all these specifications mean and why might they be important you ask?
- Correct Sizing (Length, Waist, Stance Width, Rider Weight, Flex)
It could probably be inferred from above that the a longer board rides faster and rides more stable at speed.
- Shape (True Twin, Directional Twin, Directional, Powder, Volume Shifted)
- Profile (Camber, Reverse Camber (Rocker), Flat, Hybrid)
- Extra nerdy: Efective Edge, Sidecut Radius, Edge Tech
